# Spec: STW-0001 — Build www.studytrax.com one-shot static site

## Problem Statement

Build a complete, production-grade marketing website for Studytrax — the EDC (Electronic Data Capture) research data platform. Target audience: Principal Investigators at academic medical centers, research administrators, department chairs, study coordinators, disease-foundation boards, biostatisticians, IRB reviewers. One-shot generation: produce every file, do not ask clarifying questions, do not skip sections, do not invent facts.

## §0 — Absolute Rules (violation = rejection)

1. **Never attack REDCap.** Forbidden words anywhere in the site: *unusable, ditch, frustration, overwhelming, unhappy, can't, broken, painful, nightmare, hate.* REDCap is a legitimate, institutionally endorsed tool that is the right choice for many studies. Show Studytrax's ceilings, not REDCap's floor.
2. **Never claim or imply SOC 2 Type II.** Substitute: HIPAA and ISO 27001 posture continuously monitored through Vanta. Vanta is NOT an audit firm — never describe it as one, as a pen-test vendor, or as an attestation issuer. Correct phrasings: "continuously monitored through Vanta", "HIPAA controls evidence maintained via Vanta", "ISO 27001 readiness tracked through Vanta". Forbidden: "audited by Vanta", "Vanta attestation", "Vanta-certified", "Vanta penetration test".
3. **Never guarantee or boost grant scores.** Frame grant-support as: concrete, defensible language for NIH Innovation and Approach sections, plus DMS plan implementation as a live study feature.
4. **Tailored Learning Path = specific-aims-stage only.** Every mention must include "in development" or "planned capability". Never claim existing participants have used it.
5. **AI roadmap is forward-looking only.** Short-term = expert-guided, data-driven. Long-term = AI-derived. Never "Studytrax uses AI to…" in present tense.
6. **Workbench = mature shipping feature.** Present-tense, mature-product voice. Never "planned", "in development", "upcoming", "new", or "beta". Only the Tailored Learning Path uses in-development language.
7. **Do not invent.** No numbers, quotes, testimonials, logos, case studies, publication titles, people's names, or institutional affiliations beyond what's explicitly provided. Use `<!-- [MISSING: ...] -->` comments and visible `<span class="missing">[detail pending]</span>` placeholders.
8. **Plain-English first sentence on every page.** Acronyms (EDC, LIMS, DMS, PRO, IRB, BAA, CRF, eCRF, REDCap, PCORI, NACE) spelled out on first use per page.
9. **DRY compliance content.** Tango Card architecture, audit-firm name, HIPAA BAA statement live canonically on `/compliance-security` and are referenced from other pages.
10. **Validation block ≥ ceilings block on REDCap comparison.** "When REDCap is the right call" must be at least as long/generous as "When research needs more than forms".
11. **"IF" is load-bearing** in the REDCap CTA: *"Book a 5-minute fit check — if your REDCap setup is working for what you need, we'll say so."* Do not edit it out.

## §1 — Identity & Voice

Studytrax = research data platform used by academic medical centers for clinical trials, patient registries, real-world evidence studies, and learning health networks — and to turn resulting data into funded grants and published manuscripts.

**Primary audience:** Principal Investigators at academic medical centers. Secondary: research admins, department chairs, study coordinators, disease-foundation boards. Tertiary: biostatisticians, IRB reviewers.

**Voice:** Respectful of readers' time and expertise. Specific, not salesy. Cites real academic work. Never condescends to REDCap users. Never hypes AI.

### Feature-Voice Matrix

| Feature | Voice | Evidence style | CTA pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| Participant Portal | Warm, participant-centered, concrete examples | Participant-engagement literature (PDRC LN, Cope 2020; PHE-model, Barello 2015, Graffigna 2016/2017; retention: Ali 2020) | "See what participants see" |
| Workbench | Methodical, biostatistician-friendly, manuscript-oriented | NIH 2023 DMS Policy; live-linked table examples | "Request a Workbench walkthrough" |
| Grant-Support Service | PI-to-PI, concrete deliverables, no hype | Named deliverables: support letter, budget proposal, pre-written Innovation/Dissemination text, three recruitment-model options | "Start your grant-support intake" |
| Flexible Clinical Reports | Clinician-facing, same-day-care-plan framing | 2020 Clinical Report Design Techniques blog | "See a live report example" |
| Compliance & Security | Dry, institutional, auditor-ready | ISO 27001, HIPAA BAA, 21 CFR Part 11, audit trail | "Download the compliance pack" |

## §3 — Tech Stack & Constraints

- Static HTML/CSS/vanilla JS. **No React, no Next.js, no build step.** One `index.html` per page, shared `/assets/css/site.css`, shared `/assets/js/site.js`.
- **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.2 AA. Semantic HTML5, ARIA where required, visible focus rings, skip-to-content link, alt text on every image.
- **Performance:** LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1. Inline critical CSS in `<head>`. No web fonts beyond system stack unless strictly necessary. No tracking scripts.
- **Mobile-first:** breakpoints at 640px, 960px, 1200px.

## §8 — Pricing & Licensing Page

### Block 1 — Page headline and opening copy (render VERBATIM)

> # Pricing is a conversation, not a line item.
>
> Studytrax pricing is set by the Principal Investigator's budget, not by a rate card. Every study and every registry is different, so we start with what your grant, your disease foundation, or your department can actually support — and we build the engagement around that number.
>
> To give you a sense of the range, here is where most projects land:
>
> **Studies**
>
> From around $2,000 as a one-time fee for a small, short-horizon study, up to roughly $85,000 in year one and about 20% of that in subsequent years for a fully-featured, multi-site trial with a Participant Portal and Workbench build-out. Setup hours are estimated at $125 per hour, and a no-cost REDCap transfer path is always on the table when a PI wants to evaluate Studytrax without committing a budget line.
>
> **Registries**
>
> Between $1 and $5 per participant per year, depending on registry size, governance requirements, and how much of the Participant Portal, custom clinical reports, and Workbench are in scope. Setup hours at $125 per hour.
>
> These are the ends of the range, not a published rate card. The actual number for your project is the number the PI tells us is possible — and then we scope what Studytrax can deliver inside that envelope.
>
> All pricing is subject to change and is finalized only after a scoping conversation. Nothing on this page is a quote or an offer.

### Block 2 — How licensing works

One short paragraph for a research administrator, not a procurement buyer. Cover: (a) PI-led scoping conversation as entry point; (b) annual vs. one-time structures depending on study horizon; (c) setup hours as separate line item absorbable by grant "other direct costs"; (d) free REDCap transfer path.

### Block 3 — Grant-Support Service as a named line item

SKU card on Pricing page, one-sentence summary, link to `/for-grant-writers` for full deliverable list. Do not restate the full deliverable list here. Price: *"scoped per engagement as part of the overall Studytrax pricing conversation."*

### Block 4 — Four use-case pillars at a glance

Four simple cards (Clinical Trials · Patient Registries · Real-World Evidence · Learning Health Networks), each with one-sentence description of how pricing typically shapes up for that pillar. **Not tiered plans with fixed per-seat pricing** — pillars are scoping frames, not SKUs.

### Block 5 — CTA

Primary: "Start a scoping conversation" → `/contact?topic=pricing`. Secondary: "See how Studytrax compares to REDCap" → `/compare-to-redcap`.

### Forbidden on Pricing page

- Per-seat pricing
- "Starting at $X" anchor language that locks the low end
- Any numeric claim not in Block 1 above
- Any language implying published numbers are binding quotes
